Multifaceted Minimalism
Minimalism isn’t just about just keeping only what you need, it’s also a mindset. Decluttering can be both mental and physical.
Konmari method → clothes, books, miscl, sentimental → separate things into categories → use a yes/ no/ maybe pile if you aren’t really sure of what you want to keep and what you want to get rid of. This can also be applied to relationships → toxic people, cut out negative people or at least try to limit interaction, keep people who make you happy close to you… Don’t settle for anything less than what you think you should have
Minimalism doesn’t mean that you should get rid of everything, don’t go to either extreme, only do what feels comfortable for you Minimalist color scheme → colors that you are comfortable with wearing and have around your space the most often
Capsule Wardrobe
Minimalism can be applied in any space regardless of how big or small Finding the good middle ground is one of the best places to be because
Budgeting → minimalist mindset helps you save money as opposed to spending it all, by buying only what you need you start to develop healthy spending habits there is a common rule that whenever you buy a new item of clothing you should swap it out
